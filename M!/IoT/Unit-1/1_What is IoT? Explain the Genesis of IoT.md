# What is IoT? Explain the Genesis of IoT

The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to the interconnection of physical devices such as appliances, vehicles, sensors, machines, and other objects through the internet. These devices are embedded with software, sensors, and communication technologies that enable them to collect, exchange, and process data automatically without human intervention. 

IoT creates a smart environment where devices can communicate with each other and with users in real time. It helps in automation, monitoring, control, and intelligent decision-making across different domains such as healthcare, agriculture, smart cities, industries, and transportation. 

According to the notes, IoT is:

* A network of interconnected physical objects
* Embedded with electronics and sensors
* Capable of sensing, communicating, and exchanging data
* Able to operate with minimal human interaction 

## Features of IoT

1. Connectivity – Devices connect through the internet.
2. Intelligence – Smart processing and automation.
3. Sensing – Sensors collect real-time data.
4. Communication – Devices exchange information.
5. Automation – Tasks are performed automatically.
6. Scalability – Supports billions of connected devices.

## Applications of IoT

* Smart homes
* Smart cities
* Healthcare monitoring
* Industrial automation
* Agriculture
* Traffic monitoring
* Smart energy systems

# Genesis of IoT

The genesis of IoT explains the evolution and development of connected devices over time. The concept of IoT emerged gradually through innovations in networking, embedded systems, and wireless communication. 

## Evolution of IoT

### 1. 1982 – Internet Connected Vending Machine

The first IoT-like device was a vending machine at Carnegie Mellon University connected to the internet. It could report inventory status and temperature remotely. This became the first example of remote monitoring. 

### 2. 1990 – Internet Toaster

A toaster connected to the internet was demonstrated, allowing remote control. This showed the possibility of smart home appliances. 

### 3. 1999 – Term “IoT” Coined

Kevin Ashton introduced the term “Internet of Things.” He described a system where devices communicate and exchange data automatically through the internet. 

### 4. 2000 – LG Smart Refrigerator

LG launched a smart refrigerator capable of checking and managing stored items remotely. This was an important milestone in consumer IoT. 

### 5. 2004 – Smart Watches

Wearable devices with connectivity and health tracking features introduced IoT into wearable technology. 

### 6. 2007 – Smart iPhone

Apple iPhone revolutionized IoT by enabling smartphones to act as hubs connecting multiple smart services and devices. 

### 7. 2009 – IoT in Automobiles

Vehicles started using sensors for diagnostics, monitoring, and performance analysis, leading to connected cars. 

### 8. 2011 – Smart TVs

Smart TVs introduced internet connectivity, streaming services, and interactive applications into home entertainment systems. 

### 9. 2013 – Google Lens

Google Lens demonstrated IoT-enabled image recognition and smart interaction with physical objects through mobile devices. 

### 10. 2014 – Amazon Echo

Amazon Echo with Alexa enabled voice-controlled smart homes and intelligent assistants. 

### 11. 2015 – Tesla Autopilot

Tesla introduced semi-autonomous driving using interconnected sensors and intelligent software systems.
