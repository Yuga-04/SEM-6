# Project – Definition

A project is a planned activity or organized effort undertaken to achieve a specific objective within a given period and budget. It involves the allocation of resources such as money, manpower, materials, and technology for producing goods or services. A project is created after identifying a business opportunity and analyzing its feasibility. 

According to the PDF, project identification and selection are important stages in entrepreneurship and business plan preparation. The project selected should be practically possible, technically feasible, economically viable, financially profitable, socially acceptable, and environmentally friendly. 

# Steps Involved in the Identification and Selection of a Project

Project identification and selection involve several systematic steps to choose the most suitable and profitable business opportunity.

# 1. Generation of Business Ideas

The first step is identifying business ideas from different sources. Entrepreneurs collect ideas that can be converted into successful business opportunities.

## Sources of Business Ideas:

* Customers and market surveys
* Existing businesses
* Distribution channels
* Government policies and patents
* Financial institutions and development agencies
* Research and development
* Trade fairs and exhibitions
* Brainstorming and focus groups

The PDF explains that entrepreneurial ideas should satisfy customer needs and provide utility to prospective customers. 

# 2. Preliminary Screening of Ideas

After generating many ideas, unsuitable ideas are eliminated through initial screening. The entrepreneur checks whether the idea is:

* Practically possible
* Technically feasible
* Economically viable
* Financially profitable
* Socially acceptable
* Environment friendly

Only promising ideas are selected for further analysis. 

# 3. Pre-Feasibility Study (PFS)

A Pre-Feasibility Study is a rough screening of business ideas to identify the most promising project option.

## Objectives of PFS:

* Examine all project alternatives
* Identify attractive investment opportunities
* Reduce unnecessary expenditure of time and money
* Determine whether the project is worth pursuing

The PDF states that PFS helps in making “go/no-go” decisions. 

## Areas Covered in PFS:

* Technical feasibility
* Market feasibility
* Organizational feasibility
* Financial feasibility



# 4. Market Analysis

The entrepreneur studies market conditions to understand customer demand and competition.

## Market Criteria Include:

* Market demand
* Industry structure
* Market potential
* Competition analysis
* Sales projection
* Access to market outlets

This step ensures that there is sufficient demand for the product or service. 

# 5. Technical Feasibility Analysis

Technical analysis determines whether the project can be implemented successfully using available technology and resources.

## Technical Factors:

* Availability of machinery and equipment
* Suitability of production technology
* Availability of raw materials
* Availability of labor
* Project location and infrastructure
* Power and utility requirements



# 6. Organizational Feasibility

This step evaluates the organizational requirements of the project.

## Organizational Criteria:

* Human resources and skills required
* Organizational structure
* Competency of management
* Legal form of organization
* Background of promoters

The entrepreneur ensures that proper management and skilled manpower are available for smooth operation. 

# 7. Financial Feasibility Analysis

Financial analysis determines the profitability and financial strength of the project.

## Financial Criteria:

* Profit and loss projection
* Cash flow projection
* Break-even analysis
* Projected balance sheet
* Capital requirement
* Availability of finance

The project should provide adequate returns and maintain financial stability. 

# 8. Selection of Product or Project

The entrepreneur selects the most suitable project after evaluating all alternatives.

## Criteria for Selection of Product:

* Fulfillment of customer needs
* Product differentiation
* Product acceptance
* Future market potential
* Demand-supply gap
* Government policies and support
* Overall project feasibility

The selected project should provide competitive advantage and long-term sustainability. 

# 9. Final Feasibility Study and Decision Making

A detailed feasibility study is conducted before final approval of the project. Based on technical, market, organizational, and financial analysis, the entrepreneur takes the final investment decision.

The project is accepted if:

* It is profitable
* Risks are manageable
* Market demand exists
* Resources are available
* Returns are satisfactory

Otherwise, the project may be rejected or modified. 

# Conclusion

Project identification and selection are crucial processes in entrepreneurship. A successful project must satisfy customer needs, be technically feasible, financially profitable, and economically viable. Proper feasibility analysis helps entrepreneurs minimize risks and select the most promising business opportunity for long-term success.
