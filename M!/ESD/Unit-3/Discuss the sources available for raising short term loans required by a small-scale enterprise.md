# Sources Available for Raising Short-Term Loans for a Small-Scale Enterprise

Small-scale enterprises require **short-term finance mainly to meet working capital requirements** such as purchasing raw materials, paying wages, meeting operating expenses, and maintaining inventories. Short-term loans are generally repayable within **one year**. Various sources are available for raising such finance.

## 1. Commercial Banks

Commercial banks are the **most important source of short-term finance** for small-scale enterprises. Banks provide working capital through different forms:

* **Cash Credit:** Banks allow businesses to withdraw money up to a sanctioned limit against security such as inventory or receivables.
* **Bank Overdraft:** Firms can withdraw more money than the balance available in their account up to a fixed limit.
* **Short-Term Loans:** Banks provide loans for a short period, generally up to one year, to meet urgent business requirements.

## 2. Trade Credit

Trade credit is the **credit given by suppliers of raw materials or goods**. The enterprise receives materials immediately and pays the amount later after a certain credit period.

**Advantages:**

* Easy and convenient source of finance
* No immediate cash payment required
* Helps maintain smooth production

## 3. Bank Advances

Banks provide advances in several forms to small businesses:

* **Bills Discounting:** Banks purchase bills of exchange before their maturity and provide immediate cash to the enterprise.
* **Letter of Credit:** Helps businesses obtain goods from suppliers by guaranteeing payment.

## 4. Commercial Credit from Financial Institutions

Some financial institutions provide short-term financial assistance to small-scale enterprises to support production and marketing activities.

Examples include:

* Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI)
* State Financial Corporations (SFCs)

## 5. Advances from Customers

Sometimes customers provide **advance payments** for goods or services. This advance acts as a **short-term source of finance** for the enterprise.

Benefits:

* Reduces need for borrowing
* Improves working capital position

## 6. Credit from Cooperative Banks

Small-scale enterprises can also obtain short-term loans from **cooperative banks and cooperative credit societies**. These institutions provide loans at relatively lower interest rates to encourage small business development.

## 7. Loans from Moneylenders

In urgent situations, small entrepreneurs may borrow from **private moneylenders**. Though easily available, these loans generally carry **high interest rates**, so they are used only when other sources are not available.

---

# Conclusion

Short-term loans are essential for the **working capital needs of small-scale enterprises**. Sources such as **commercial banks, trade credit, financial institutions, customer advances, cooperative banks, and moneylenders** help businesses maintain smooth operations and meet day-to-day expenses. Proper selection of financing sources ensures better liquidity and efficient business management.
