# **Prevention and Rehabilitation of Sick Business Units (Critical Evaluation)**

## **Introduction**

Business sickness can severely affect the survival of small enterprises. Therefore, both **preventive measures** (to avoid sickness) and **rehabilitation methods** (to revive sick units) are essential. A critical evaluation helps understand their effectiveness and limitations.

---

# **I. Prevention of Business Sickness**

## **1. Proper Financial Management**

* Adequate working capital planning
* Efficient budgeting and cost control
* Timely repayment of loans

**Evaluation:**
✔ Helps maintain liquidity and stability
✘ Small firms often lack financial expertise

---

## **2. Efficient Management and Planning**

* Skilled leadership and decision-making
* Long-term and short-term planning
* Adoption of modern management techniques

**Evaluation:**
✔ Reduces operational risks
✘ Depends heavily on the entrepreneur’s capability

---

## **3. Market Research and Strategy**

* Understanding customer needs
* Competitive pricing and promotion
* Strong distribution channels

**Evaluation:**
✔ Improves sales and competitiveness
✘ Costly and difficult for small enterprises

---

## **4. Technological Upgradation**

* Use of modern machinery
* Automation and innovation

**Evaluation:**
✔ Enhances productivity and quality
✘ Requires high investment

---

## **5. Human Resource Development**

* Training and skill development
* Employee motivation and retention

**Evaluation:**
✔ Improves efficiency and morale
✘ Training costs may be high

---

## **6. Government Support**

* Subsidies, incentives, and policy support
* Access to credit schemes

**Evaluation:**
✔ Provides financial relief
✘ Bureaucratic delays reduce effectiveness

---

# **II. Rehabilitation of Sick Business Units**

## **1. Financial Restructuring**

* Rescheduling of loans
* Reduction in interest rates
* Infusion of fresh capital

**Evaluation:**
✔ Provides immediate relief
✘ May increase long-term financial burden

---

## **2. Managerial Restructuring**

* Change in management
* Hiring professional managers

**Evaluation:**
✔ Brings new ideas and efficiency
✘ Resistance from existing owners

---

## **3. Operational Restructuring**

* Improving production efficiency
* Cost reduction techniques
* Better inventory control

**Evaluation:**
✔ Enhances productivity
✘ Requires time to show results

---

## **4. Marketing Improvements**

* Rebranding and repositioning
* Expanding market reach

**Evaluation:**
✔ Helps regain market share
✘ Success not guaranteed in competitive markets

---

## **5. Government Rehabilitation Schemes**

* Revival packages for MSMEs
* Financial assistance and tax relief

**Evaluation:**
✔ Supports struggling units
✘ Implementation gaps and delays

---

## **6. Mergers and Takeovers**

* Sick units merged with healthy firms

**Evaluation:**
✔ Ensures survival of business
✘ Loss of independence

---

# **III. Critical Analysis**

* Preventive measures are **more effective** than rehabilitation
* Early detection of sickness is crucial
* Many rehabilitation methods provide **temporary relief** rather than permanent solutions
* Lack of coordination between financial institutions and government agencies reduces effectiveness
* Small enterprises face **resource constraints**, limiting both prevention and recovery

---

## **Conclusion**

Prevention and rehabilitation are both essential strategies to tackle business sickness. While preventive measures ensure long-term sustainability, rehabilitation helps revive struggling units. However, their success depends on timely implementation, efficient management, and strong institutional support.
