## Enumerate the Major Problems Faced by Small Scale Enterprises (15 Marks)

Small Scale Industries (SSI) play an important role in the economic development of a country. They generate employment opportunities, promote regional development, and utilize local resources. However, small-scale enterprises face several problems that affect their growth and efficiency. 

---

# 1. Financial Problems

Small-scale enterprises often face **shortage of capital and financial resources**. It is difficult for them to obtain loans from banks and financial institutions due to lack of security and creditworthiness.

---

# 2. Raw Material Problems

Small industries face difficulty in obtaining **quality raw materials at reasonable prices**. They often depend on middlemen and receive raw materials irregularly.

---

# 3. Marketing Problems

Small enterprises usually face problems in **marketing their products** because they lack proper advertising facilities, distribution channels, and market information.

---

# 4. Technological Problems

Many small industries use **outdated technology and machinery**, which leads to low productivity and poor product quality.

---

# 5. Managerial Problems

Small-scale enterprises are often managed by owners who may lack **professional management skills and technical knowledge**, leading to inefficient management.

---

# 6. Labour Problems

Small industries face difficulties in getting **skilled and trained workers**. Labour turnover is also high because employees prefer to work in large industries.

---

# 7. Competition from Large Industries

Small-scale industries face **strong competition from large-scale industries** that have better financial resources, technology, and marketing networks.

---

# 8. Infrastructure Problems

Inadequate infrastructure such as **power supply, transportation, communication, and storage facilities** affects the smooth functioning of small enterprises.

---

# 9. Lack of Research and Development

Small industries often lack **research and development facilities**, which makes it difficult for them to innovate and improve product quality.

---

# Conclusion

Although small-scale enterprises contribute significantly to economic development, they face several challenges such as **financial constraints, raw material shortages, marketing difficulties, technological limitations, and competition from large industries**. Proper government support, improved infrastructure, and better management practices can help overcome these problems.
