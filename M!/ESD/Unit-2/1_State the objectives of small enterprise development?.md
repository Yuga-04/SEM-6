# Objectives of Small Enterprise Development

Small Enterprise Development aims at promoting the growth and development of small-scale industries (SSI/MSME) in the country. These objectives help in improving employment opportunities, balanced regional development, and economic growth. According to the PDF, the important objectives are: 

## 1. Generation of Immediate Employment with Low Investment

Small enterprises require comparatively less capital investment and are capable of generating large employment opportunities quickly. They help reduce unemployment and underemployment, especially in rural and semi-urban areas.

## 2. Promotion of Equitable Distribution of National Income

Small enterprises reduce concentration of wealth by distributing income among a larger number of people. They encourage decentralized industrial growth and help achieve economic equality in society.

## 3. Effective Mobilization of Untapped Capital and Human Skills

Small enterprises utilize idle savings, local talent, and human resources effectively. They encourage entrepreneurship and make productive use of available manpower and local resources.

## 4. Dispersal of Manufacturing Activities

Small-scale industries help spread industrial activities across different regions instead of concentrating industries only in urban areas. This supports balanced regional development and reduces regional disparities.

## 5. Development of Rural and Backward Areas

Small enterprises promote industrialization in rural and backward regions by utilizing local resources and creating employment opportunities, thereby improving living standards.

## 6. Encouragement of Entrepreneurship

Small enterprises motivate individuals to become entrepreneurs by providing opportunities for self-employment, innovation, and independent business activities.

## 7. Increase in Industrial Production and Exports

SSI/MSME sectors contribute significantly to manufacturing output and exports. They support economic development by increasing production and foreign exchange earnings.

## 8. Optimum Utilization of Local Resources

Small enterprises make efficient use of local raw materials, skills, and traditional techniques which might otherwise remain unused.

## 9. Reduction in Migration to Urban Areas

By creating employment in villages and small towns, small enterprises help reduce migration of people to metropolitan cities in search of jobs.

## 10. Support to Large Industries

Small enterprises often act as ancillary units supplying components, spare parts, and services to large industries, thereby supporting industrial growth.

## Conclusion

Small Enterprise Development plays a vital role in economic and social development by generating employment, promoting entrepreneurship, ensuring balanced regional growth, and improving income distribution. Hence, governments provide various incentives, subsidies, and support schemes for the growth of SSI and MSME sectors.
