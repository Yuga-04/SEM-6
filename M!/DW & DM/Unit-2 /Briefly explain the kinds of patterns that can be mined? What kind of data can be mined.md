# Kinds of Patterns that Can Be Mined and Kinds of Data that Can Be Mined (15 Marks)

## 1. Kinds of Patterns that Can Be Mined

Data mining functionalities are used to discover **interesting patterns and relationships from large databases**. 

### 1. Concept/Class Description

Concept description describes the **general characteristics of a target class of data**.

It includes:

**a) Data Characterization**
Summarizes the characteristics of a class of data.
Example: Studying the characteristics of customers whose sales increased last year. 

**b) Data Discrimination**
Compares the features of one class of data objects with another class.
Example: Comparing customers who buy expensive products with those who buy cheaper products. 

---

### 2. Mining Frequent Patterns, Associations and Correlations

Frequent patterns are patterns that **occur frequently in data**.

Example association rule:

```
buys(X, "computer") → buys(X, "software")
```

This indicates that customers who buy computers also tend to buy software. 

---

### 3. Classification

Classification is the process of **finding a model that predicts the class label of objects** based on training data. 

Example: Classifying emails as **spam or non-spam**.

---

### 4. Prediction

Prediction is used to **predict continuous numeric values**.

Example: Predicting **future sales or stock prices**. 

---

### 5. Clustering

Clustering groups data objects into clusters where **objects in the same cluster are similar** and objects in different clusters are dissimilar. 

Example: Grouping customers based on purchasing behavior.

📄 Diagram: **Customer cluster diagram – Page 47 (Figure 1.11)**.

---

### 6. Outlier Analysis

Outliers are **data objects that differ significantly from other objects**. 

Example: Detecting **fraudulent credit card transactions**.

---

### 7. Evolution Analysis

Evolution analysis describes **patterns and trends over time**. 

Example: Analyzing **stock market trends or seasonal sales**.

---

# 2. Kinds of Data that Can Be Mined

Data mining can be applied to many types of databases and information repositories.

### 1. Relational Databases

These databases consist of **tables with rows and columns** storing structured data. 

Example: Customer database.

---

### 2. Data Warehouses

A **data warehouse** stores integrated data from multiple sources for analysis and decision making. 

📄 Diagram: **Typical data warehouse framework – Page 15 (Figure 1.7)**.

---

### 3. Transactional Databases

Transactional databases store **transaction records such as sales or purchases**. 

Example: Market basket data.

---

### 4. Object-Relational Databases

These databases store **complex objects combining relational and object-oriented models**. 

---

### 5. Spatial and Spatiotemporal Databases

These databases store **geographical or location-based data** such as maps and satellite images. 

---

### 6. Time-Series Databases

These databases store **data collected over time**.

Example: Weather data or stock prices. 

---

### 7. Text and Multimedia Databases

Text databases store **documents**, while multimedia databases store **images, audio and video data**. 

---

### 8. Web Databases

These databases contain **data from web pages and internet sources**, which can be mined to analyze user behavior. 

---

# Conclusion

Data mining can discover many patterns such as **classification, clustering, association rules, prediction and outlier detection**. These patterns can be mined from various data sources including **relational databases, data warehouses, transactional databases, spatial data, multimedia data and web data**, helping organizations gain valuable insights.
